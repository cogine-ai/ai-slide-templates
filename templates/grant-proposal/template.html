Grant proposal

Community Learning Fund

Prepared for the Horizon Civic Foundation by Northside Learning Lab.

Request summary

Expanding after-school access for middle-school students.

Funding request: $480,000 over 24 months.

Executive summary

Why this proposal matters now

Northside Learning Lab is requesting two years of support to expand evidence-based tutoring, mentorship, and family engagement for students in grades 6-8.

The program targets three outcomes: improved math proficiency, stronger attendance, and higher student confidence before the transition to high school.

Program model

Inputs move through three measurable activity streams.

Evidence base

The approach combines academic support and belonging.

Academic lift

Students in pilot groups gained an average 11 percentile points after 16 weeks.

School partner

"The model gives students consistent adults and clear practice time after the bell."

Attendance

Mentor reminders reduced missed program days by 29% in the spring pilot.

Family trust

Evening workshops increased caregiver participation without adding school burden.

Risk controls

Delivery risks and mitigations

  • Recruitment risk is addressed through school counselor referrals and family information nights.
  • Staffing risk is reduced by maintaining a trained substitute tutor pool.
  • Data quality risk is managed through one shared reporting calendar and monthly review.
  • Sustainability risk is reduced by documenting site playbooks before expansion.

Partnerships

Sustainability

How the program continues after the grant

By month 18, the team will secure district cost-share commitments, renew local business sponsorships, and package the operating model for additional philanthropic partners.

The proposal includes a documented curriculum, training plan, and reporting template so each site can continue with lower central support.

Decision request

Approve $480,000 to serve 360 students over two years.
